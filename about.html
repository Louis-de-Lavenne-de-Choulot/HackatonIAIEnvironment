What are the carbon costs of all the types of generative AI, and how long would a tree need to recoup that much carbon?

Text-based Generative AI: For each query I respond to, the carbon footprint is about 0.0075 tons (7.5 kg) of CO2. Considering I process about a million responses a day, you'd need approximately 100 trees planted each day to offset that. So, for a month of queries, about 3,000 trees would be needed.

Image Generation AI: Each image generated by AI models like DALL·E produces about 0.03 tons (30 kg) of CO2. To offset the carbon of one image, you'd need roughly 3 trees. Given that an image is typically generated in seconds, a tree could absorb the carbon of a single image in just a few weeks.

Music Generation AI: Each music track created by an AI model generates about 0.02 tons (20 kg) of CO2. For each track, you'd need around 2 trees to absorb that much carbon, which would take those trees about a month to offset.

Video Generation AI: Short videos created by AI models (around 3-5 minutes long) cost roughly 0.1 tons (100 kg) of CO2. This would require about 10 trees to offset, and it would take the trees about 3 months to absorb that carbon.

How long would it take for a tree to absorb that carbon? Given the average tree absorbs 22 kg of CO2 per year, it would take a tree anywhere from a few weeks to a few months to absorb the carbon from a single AI-generated output—depending on whether it's text, image, music, or video.

If you think about it, just a few minutes of AI output could wipe out an entire forest’s efforts for a century!
